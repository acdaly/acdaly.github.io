teamLab River Prototype


Made with Unity

As a teamLab interactive team intern, I was tasked with creating a new projection prototype for the Aerial Climbing exhibit at their Borderless Museum in Tokyo. The exhibit features paths of connected swing-like boards. Visitors can walk along these suspended paths, and the surrounding projection will change in response to their movements in real time.



^ The Aerial Climbing exhibit with the original projection

The projected birds currently avoid the visitors, but this interaction is nonobvious. My proposal for the content was instead to create a river on the paths where users walk. The flow of the river increases as more people walk along the given path, and decreases overtime if no one visits it.



Each visitor is given a particular color when they enter the space. Their color will flow through the river ahead of them. As they walk, flowers will bloom along the sides of the river, and then disperse as they pass.

The visitors are simulated through the movements of the blue capsule. The river flow changes based off of the visitor/capsule position and velocity. I worked individually to conceptualize and program the prototype. The flower assets were created previously by teamLab, but all other elements of the prototype were created from scratch.
