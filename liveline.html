The Challenge

Modernizing the substance addiction recovery experience

About 1 in 13 people in the US have a substance use disorder, yet only 1 in 8 of those that needed substance use treatment recieved treatment in a specialized facility.

As part of our MHCI Capstone, the non-profit Oasis Recovery Center and start-up Biomotivate challenged us to create a new digital service to make addiction resources and support more accessible.

Context

COVID-19 exacerbates the inaccessibility of addiction resources for young adults

1 out of every 7 adults aged 18-25 in the US had a substance use disorder in 2017 - nearly double the percentage of adults over age 25 - with numbers increasing in 2019 and in 2020 due to COVID.

In-person support group meetings such as Alcoholics Anonymous are the most common form of support for addiction, but we found that young adults are more likely to feel uncomfortable attending these meetings even prior to COVID-19.

Solution

LiveLine

supports those in recovery anytime, anywhere with livestreaming

LiveLine addreses these needs:

Relevancy

Content that is applicable to someone’s beliefs, background, and circumstances.

Clarity

Gaining an understanding of one’s underlying triggers and reasons for substance use.

Community

Supportive and empathetic peers that may also connect with outside interests.

Immediacy

Having accessible resources and support at any given time and circumstance.

our prototype

Relevancy & Community | Livestreaming, Comments, Friends

Whether it be a peer support group meeting, meditation session, or guided art therapy, people can watch content that resonates with them.

Through direct search, recommendations, or comments on videos, LiveLine users can connect as friends for more direct communication. By becoming friends, you can send direct messages and see each others' public activity, such as short mood statuses, shared videos, or journal entries.

Immediacy | SOS Resources

Recovery includes managing cravings and triggers. An SOS option allows users to call out to multiple close friends simultaneously via direct message, call 911 or recovery support hotlines, and watch a playlist of pre-recorded content to help move past cravings. The SOS option is available even without having a LiveLine account for immediate support.

Clarity | Journaling and Moodtracking

Moods can easily be tracked by inputting a mood and a comment for the reason. Moods can then be viewed by month, week, or day to better understand situational triggers, emotional events, and positive progress.

There’s additionally a journaling feature where LiveLine users can write free-form daily entries or fill out recovery-focused prompts. Notes taken during live or pre-recorded videos are saved to the journal as well for easy access.

User Feedback & next steps
