overview

The Challenge

The Social Cybersecurity research group in Carnegie Mellon recruited us to create an intervention that would improve cybersecurity behaviors.

Despite cyber hacks are becoming more sophisticated and frequent, adoption of good cybersecurity habits is severely lagging behind. Google reports less than 10% of people use 2-factor authentication despite it being recommended as best practice.

The Social Cybersecurity team wanted to get new ideas on how they could tackle this social issue. We met their challenge by designing an "adulting" challenge in the form of a mobile app.

Our Scope

We had 10 weeks to design an intervention.

This project for the Social Cybersecurity group was done in the context of our 10-week final project for CMU’s Persuasive Design course (we were the only group to work with them for the class).

Our concept would need to:

    1.    Increase adoption of good cybersecurity practices.
    2.    Be willingly used by non-cybersecurity experts.
    3.    Be a novel idea to inspire new research.

Our Solution

The 30-Day Adulting Challenge

Our mobile app intermixes cybersecurity with other tasks that are considered good “adulting” practices. Each day tackles a different adulting subject, such as cleaning, sleep hygiene, and finances. With fun color palette, illustrations, and copywriting, we plan to target a young adult demographic.

We use psychology techniques in our app including:

Intermixing

Mixing content with other topics makes the true persuasive intentions less obvious (and more effective)

Theory of Planned Behavior

One’s intentions, social norms, and how easy the behavior seems are predictors of behavioral

Social Proof

People often act as they others do (particularly if they are unsure of what to do)

Intermixing

Different topics covered each day

Each day focuses on one “adulting” topic. While some days may focus on cybersecurity, we intermix in other topics such as cleaning, style, nutrition, finance, and more.

theory of planned behavior

Changing Societal Norms to Change Behavior

By including cybersecurity topics in this challenge, we hope to make it a society norm to consider cybersecurity a part of adulting (which the Theory of Planned Behavior suggests in turn would change cybersecurity behavior).

Social Proof

Motivation with Social Proof

Friends can be linked on the app. When they complete a task, it’ll show up on the Social feed. This can help motivate users to continue, as well as spread word of the challenge.

Our Prototype

Complete at least 1 of the 3 tasks per day

The task card can be clicked to see a more detailed description and mark it as complete. At least 1 of the 3 tasks needs to be completed in order to progress in the challenge.

Click “I Already Do This” to get new tasks

Some people may already do the tasks we have listed, and in that case they can get a new and more challenging replacement.

    reflection

    Dividing and Conquering Within a Team

    I learned that it can be extremely effective to clearly divide a team to tackle different components of a project. Because we all had separate roles, it was much easier to coordinate and tackle the project.

    To improve on our process, I would try to create a timeline for our project earlier on. Especially when one pair’s work affects another, it’s important to understand when we expect work from each other and as a larger team. Overall, I felt we had a very healthy team dynamic and am proud of the work we made together.
